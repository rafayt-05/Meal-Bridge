How MealBridge works

Three steps — donors post, NGOs claim, food reaches people in need before it spoils.

1

Donors post surplus

Restaurants, caterers and households list what's available — quantity, pickup window, photo and exact location pinned on a map.

2

Nearby NGOs see it instantly

Verified NGOs in the area see the offer on a live map with distance from their base. They pick the ones that fit their route and capacity.

3

Food reaches people

The NGO collects from the donor and distributes to shelters, community kitchens or directly to people in need. Tracked end-to-end.

Our mission

Bridge the gap between surplus and scarcity.

Every day, restaurants pack up perfectly good food at closing time. Caterers finish events with trays of untouched meals. Households cook more than they can eat. At the same time, shelters and community kitchens are stretched thin.

MealBridge sits in the middle as a free third-party connector — donors don't need to find an NGO, and NGOs don't need to chase donations. The platform does the matching by location, in real time.

  • Zero fees for donors and NGOs — forever.
  • Verified NGOs only, so donors know food reaches the right hands.
  • Live distance matching means food gets there before it spoils.
  • Built as a not-for-profit; data is never sold.
